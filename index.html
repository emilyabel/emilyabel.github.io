Emily Abel, M.S.

Doctoral Candidate - Human Development & Family Studies - Purdue University

I am a PhD candidate in Human Development and Family Studies at Purdue University with a concentration in Psychological Statistics. I expect to receive my PhD in May 2019.

I am currently seeking a summer internship in data analytics (people analytics with a research focus preferred) where I can apply my advanced methodology skills to help companies make informed decisions to improve employee retention, productivity, satisfaction, and overall health and well-being. With my background in Human Development and Family Studies, I will help companies make these decisions while leveraging the context of the family--which both affects and is affected by workplace decisions.

My research background is in sleep health, particularly within the context of the dynamic family system. Through this work, I use several advanced analytic techniques to address substantive questions such as how poor sleep affects behavior the following day, how changes in sleep relate to changes in behavior over time (including models with cortisol mediating these changes), and how sleep relates to other health concerns (such as BMI) and family processes (such as parenting behaviors, child outcomes, parental stress, and psychological well-being).

Procedural Music Generator

Imagine that you were to list the greatest composers who ever existed. One might list greats like Mozart, Beethoven, or Bach. Imagine a future in which that list is instead comprised of algorithms and AI. This project comes nowhere near that future, but it's a young programmer's start to that dream.

This Procedural Music Generator takes a small selection of parameters such as genre and tempo, and uses those along with a seed to generate one of a near endless combinations of songs.

Buggy's TV Guide

Buggy's TV Guide is a simple desktop JAVA application for creating lists of all of your favorite upcoming shows. It allows you to search for your favorite shows, see all of their upcoming episodes, learn about the actors, and make your own TV guide. You can export your lists to iCal or share what you're watching through Facebook or Twitter.
